What we're looking for

We hire improvement-minded people with a competitive spirit who take pride in their work. If you're passionate about building great products, thrive in a fast-paced environment, and want to win, this is the place.

We're engineers, traders, and operators who think markets are fascinating. You'll work at the intersection of finance, tech, and real-time systems. We celebrate wins, learn from failures, and genuinely like each other.

You'll thrive here if…

  • You want to do genuinely great work, not just keep busy
  • You like being part of a small, intentionally lean team where your work matters
  • You value complete internal transparency — we share financials daily
  • You want high autonomy paired with high expectations
  • You're comfortable setting your own salary based on the value you deliver
  • You enjoy shared ownership — everyone does customer support rotations

This probably isn't for you if…

  • You need detailed instructions and close supervision
  • You prefer large, structured organisations with clear hierarchies
  • You're looking for a place that prioritises comfort over performance
  • You think "that's not my job" when you see a problem
  • You'd rather optimise process than ship product

How we build & work

We build everything ourselves. The matching engine, the market maker, the risk system, the frontends — all in-house. Owning the full stack is what lets us move fast and compound our advantage.

Exchange infrastructure: Rust. Frontends: React. Market-making algorithms: proprietary. We use Kafka for event streaming, PostgreSQL for persistence, and run everything on Kubernetes. There's no outsourced core — every critical system is written and operated by the team in London.

How we celebrate

Work is important. So is not working.

We work hard. We also know how to turn it off. We eat together every day, free lunch from our in-house chef. It's not a perk, it's how we connect. We talk about work, life, and occasionally argue about why we lost money that week.

Every quarter we host all-hands meetings, every month we host townhalls where teams present their wins. We celebrate major launches, recognise great work, and roast each other's bugs. Summer parties, Christmas dinners, and an internal hackathon round out the year.

We compete: poker nights, sports watch parties, gaming tournaments. We travel together for off-sites. We occasionally commit to questionable karaoke.

The work matters. So does not working. If you're going to spend 40+ hours a week with people, you should actually like them. We do.
