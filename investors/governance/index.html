Governance Principles

As a private company, we are not required to comply with a formal corporate governance code. We choose to operate with the transparency and discipline that we believe our shareholders, regulators, and customers expect.

Board oversight

The board meets regularly to review financial performance, strategic direction, risk management, and regulatory compliance. Major capital allocation decisions, new market entries, and senior appointments require board approval.

Regulatory compliance

Smarkets holds licences in 6 jurisdictions. Each licence imposes its own governance, reporting, and operational requirements. Our compliance function reports directly to the board on regulatory matters, and we maintain ongoing dialogue with every regulator under which we operate.

Risk management

We operate an internal risk framework covering market risk (through Hanson's automated position management), operational risk (infrastructure resilience, business continuity), credit risk (customer deposits), and regulatory risk (licence conditions, jurisdictional changes).

Financial controls

Smarkets Limited files audited annual accounts with Companies House in accordance with the Companies Act 2006 and applicable UK accounting standards. Our accounts are audited by an independent external firm. Annual reports from 2015 onwards are available on our investors page.

Transparency

We operate an open salary policy internally, publish our financial performance externally, and aim to be straightforward with shareholders, employees, and customers about how the business is performing and where it's headed.

Shareholder rights

As a private limited company, our shareholder base is composed of the founding team, employees with vested equity, and institutional investors. Shareholders receive regular updates on financial performance and material business developments, and have the rights and protections afforded under the Companies Act 2006.

Responsible Betting

As an operator licensed by the UK Gambling Commission and other regulators, responsible betting is a core obligation — not an afterthought.

Our approach

We design our products to give customers control. SBK and the Smarkets exchange both include mandatory deposit limits, self-exclusion tools, reality checks, and account activity statements. We participate in GAMSTOP (the UK's national self-exclusion scheme) and share data with the Gambling Commission as required.

Our prediction market model is structurally different from traditional bookmaking: we don't profit when customers lose. On the exchange, we match buyers with sellers and charge a small commission. This means our commercial incentives are aligned with sustainable, long-term customer activity rather than maximising individual losses.

For more information, see our responsible betting resources (opens in new tab) or contact support@smarkets.com.

Code of Conduct

Smarkets expects all employees, directors, and contractors to act with integrity, comply with applicable laws and regulations, and treat colleagues, customers, and partners fairly.

Anti-bribery & corruption

We maintain a zero-tolerance policy towards bribery and corruption in line with the UK Bribery Act 2010. All employees receive training on anti-bribery obligations, and our compliance team monitors for potential violations.

Anti-money laundering

As a licensed gambling operator, we are subject to the Money Laundering Regulations 2017 and the Proceeds of Crime Act 2002. We operate a risk-based AML programme including customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting to the National Crime Agency.

Data protection

We process personal data in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018. Our privacy policy is publicly available and we maintain a data protection officer function.
