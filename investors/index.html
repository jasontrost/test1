Why Smarkets

Profitable

We've been consistently profitable for multiple years — named "one of the most profitable per capita companies in Europe" (opens in new tab) by Corporate Rebels. Our prediction market model generates strong unit economics without heavy capital requirements.

18 years of execution

Founded in 2008, we've outlasted competitors and survived every market cycle. That kind of longevity in a fast-moving industry isn't luck — it's a fundamentally sound model and a team that knows how to execute.

Three products, one engine

SBK captures retail demand through a simple sportsbook experience. The Smarkets exchange serves professional traders. Hanson — our proprietary market maker — prices everything across both products, generating high-margin revenue with a small, specialised team. It also operates on third-party platforms, meaning Smarkets earns revenue even when competitors grow.

Engineering-led

65% of our 100+ person team are engineers. We build everything ourselves — exchange infrastructure in Rust, real-time frontends in React, and proprietary market-making algorithms. This gives us a compounding technical advantage.

Regulated in five jurisdictions

Licensed by the UK Gambling Commission (opens in new tab), Malta Gaming Authority (opens in new tab), and in the US (Indiana), Ireland, and Sweden. CFTC approval pending for broader US market access.

Capital efficient growth

Better pricing attracts more order flow. More order flow creates deeper liquidity. Deeper liquidity enables even tighter pricing. This flywheel compounds without proportional increases in headcount or marketing spend — Hanson's pricing engine improves with every trade it processes, and the same infrastructure serves every product and geography.

How we make money

Hanson (market making)

Our proprietary market maker, Hanson, quotes prices on both sides of thousands of markets simultaneously. It earns the spread between buy and sell prices — generating high gross margins with a small, specialised team. Hanson also trades on third-party platforms, creating a revenue stream that's independent of our own exchange volume.

SBK (sportsbook)

SBK is our consumer sportsbook, offering better odds than traditional bookmakers by passing on the exchange's sharper pricing. Revenue comes from the edge built into each price. Customer acquisition costs run significantly below US industry averages.

Exchange fees

We charge approximately 25 basis points per trade on the Smarkets exchange — among the lowest in the industry. Professional traders and API users pay transaction fees on matched volume.

Where we're headed

As we expand into new geographies and asset classes, the model scales: Hanson's pricing engine works in any venue, SBK's format replicates into new jurisdictions, and the exchange infrastructure supports additional products without rebuilding.

Our margin advantage

Most betting companies charge customers 10–12% in hidden margin. We charge around 5%. Our prediction market model means we don't take the other side of every bet — we match buyers with sellers and let the market set the price.
