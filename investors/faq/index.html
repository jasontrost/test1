Smarkets operates two customer-facing products. The Smarkets exchange is a prediction market where users trade on the outcomes of real-world events — sports, politics, entertainment — by buying and selling contracts. SBK is a consumer sportsbook that offers better odds than traditional bookmakers by pricing through the exchange. Our proprietary market-making engine quotes prices on both sides of thousands of markets simultaneously, earning the spread.

Both products share the same underlying technology infrastructure, meaning we can enter new markets and asset classes without rebuilding from scratch. For more detail, see our About page and Technology page.

Revenue comes from three sources. Market making earns the spread between buy and sell prices across thousands of markets — this is our principal revenue stream and operates with a specialised team. SBK (sportsbook) earns revenue from the edge built into each price, with customer acquisition costs that have compared favourably with many sportsbook operators. Exchange fees are approximately 2% of net profit per market.

A betting exchange and a prediction market are interchangeable terms often used to describe marketplaces where buyers and sellers trade contracts on event outcomes. They remove the traditional bookmaker from the equation, and produce prices that reflect the market's collective probability estimate for an event. The difference is one of language. "Betting exchange" comes from the betting industry in the UK; "prediction market" comes from finance and academia with more of a US angle.

The Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) regulates event contracts in the United States. Smarkets has applied for approval to operate a prediction market for event contracts — allowing US residents to trade on the outcomes of real-world events under federal regulation. If approved, this would open a substantial new market for the company. Several other firms have received CFTC approval for specific event categories, and the regulatory framework continues to evolve. The timeline for any regulatory determination is uncertain.
