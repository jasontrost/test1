Core systems

Four interconnected systems that power every trade on the platform.

Matching engine

The heart of the exchange. Written in Rust for performance and memory safety. Processes orders with sub-100ms latency end-to-end, handles concurrent markets across sports, politics, and economics, and maintains strict price-time priority ordering. Designed for correctness first, speed second — because in financial systems, getting the answer wrong fast is worse than getting it right slowly.

Market maker

Our proprietary market-making system provides continuous liquidity across thousands of markets simultaneously. It quotes competitive two-way prices, manages inventory risk in real-time, and adapts to market conditions algorithmically. This is what makes the exchange usable — without a market maker, an order book is just an empty shell. We call it Hanson — and it's significant enough to warrant its own section below.

Risk system

Every trade, every position, every customer balance is monitored in real-time. The risk system enforces exposure limits, detects anomalous trading patterns, and manages settlement across all markets. It's integrated directly into the matching engine — risk checks happen pre-trade, not after the fact.

Payments & settlement

Deposits, withdrawals, and settlement across six jurisdictions with different regulatory requirements. Built to handle the complexity of multi-currency, multi-regulator operations without manual intervention. Every penny is accounted for in real-time.

What it does

Hanson quotes competitive two-way prices across every market on the Smarkets exchange and SBK sportsbook simultaneously. It manages inventory risk in real-time, adapts to live in-play events algorithmically, and provides the liquidity that makes both products usable. Without Hanson, the exchange is an empty order book and the sportsbook has no pricing engine.

The flywheel

Better pricing attracts more order flow. More order flow creates deeper liquidity. Deeper liquidity enables tighter pricing. This cycle compounds — and because Hanson improves with every trade it processes, the advantage widens over time. The same pricing engine serves retail sportsbook customers and professional exchange traders, so each side of the business reinforces the other.

Platform-agnostic

Hanson isn't locked to the Smarkets exchange. It operates on third-party platforms, generating revenue wherever there are markets to price. When other venues grow, Hanson can participate in that growth without requiring separate licenses, marketing spend, or local teams. This turns competitor success into an additional revenue stream.

Why it's hard to replicate

Sports and event pricing has no standardised instruments, limited historical data for one-off events, and the complexity of live in-play markets where conditions change by the second. Hanson has been refined through 11 years of continuous operation and billions of dollars in traded volume. Building something equivalent requires deep domain expertise, proprietary data, and years of iteration — not just capital.
