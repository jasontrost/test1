Core systems

Four interconnected systems that power every trade on the platform.

Matching engine

The heart of the exchange. Written in Rust for performance and memory safety. Processes orders with sub-100ms latency end-to-end, handles concurrent markets across sports, politics, and economics, and maintains strict price-time priority ordering. Designed for correctness first, speed second — because in financial systems, getting the answer wrong fast is worse than getting it right slowly.

Market maker

Our proprietary market-making system (Hanson) provides continuous liquidity across thousands of markets simultaneously. It quotes competitive two-way prices, manages inventory risk in real-time, and adapts to market conditions algorithmically. This is what makes the exchange usable — without a market maker, an order book is just an empty shell.

Risk system

Every trade, every position, every customer balance is monitored in real-time. The risk system enforces exposure limits, detects anomalous trading patterns, and manages settlement across all markets. It's integrated directly into the matching engine — risk checks happen pre-trade, not after the fact.

Payments & settlement

Deposits, withdrawals, and settlement across six jurisdictions with different regulatory requirements. Built to handle the complexity of multi-currency, multi-regulator operations without manual intervention. Every penny is accounted for in real-time.

How it fits together

Two products, one engine. Both Smarkets (the exchange) and SBK (the sportsbook) run on the same underlying infrastructure.

Why we build everything ourselves

Speed of iteration

When you own the full stack, you can ship a pricing change, a new market type, or a risk adjustment without waiting for a vendor. Our matching engine and market maker are deployed multiple times a week.

Compounding advantage

Every improvement to the matching engine benefits both the exchange and the sportsbook simultaneously. Every optimisation to the market maker improves pricing for every customer. The shared infrastructure creates a flywheel that gets stronger with every iteration.

Regulatory control

Operating in six jurisdictions means compliance isn't something you bolt on — it has to be woven into the platform. Owning the full stack lets us implement jurisdiction-specific rules at the infrastructure level, not as afterthoughts.
