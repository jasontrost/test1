What we do

Our prediction market lets people trade on the outcomes of real-world events - elections, sports, economics - at prices set by the market, not by a bookmaker. Our sportsbook, SBK, takes the same sharp pricing and wraps it in a simple betting experience.

Together, the three products serve different audiences with the same underlying engine. SBK builds retail order flow. The exchange serves professional traders. Hanson — our proprietary market maker, built over 11 years — prices everything and provides the liquidity that makes both products work. It also operates on third-party platforms, generating revenue independent of any single venue.

Why we exist

Most betting companies build in massive hidden margins, block winning players, and optimise for addiction over fairness. We think markets work better when they're transparent. When a trader can see real-time pricing, trade in and out of positions, and trust that the platform won't ban them for being good at it. That's the model we're building.

Most betting companies charge 10–12% in hidden margin. We charge ~5%. We don't take the other side of every bet, we match buyers and sellers. Better odds for customers. Profit for us. It works.

How it started

Jason Trost

Founder & CEO

“I left a UBS trading desk in 2008 because sports betting felt broken. The dominant exchange was charging 5% commission with clunky UX. I thought: if we treat bets like tradable assets instead of entertainment, we can offer 2% commission and better tech. People said it was insane to go after a monopoly. Seven years later, we were profitable. We've now processed over $47B in volume.”
