Smarkets is a prediction market exchange, founded in 2008 and headquartered in London. We run two products — the Smarkets exchange and the SBK sportsbook — both powered by the same proprietary technology.

What we do

Our exchange lets people trade on the outcomes of real-world events — elections, sports, economics — at prices set by the market, not by a bookmaker. Our sportsbook, SBK, takes the same sharp pricing and wraps it in a simple betting experience.

Together, the two products serve different audiences with the same underlying engine: a matching platform, a market maker, and a risk system — all built in-house.

Why it matters

Most betting companies charge customers 10–12% in hidden margin. We charge around 5%. That's possible because our exchange model means we don't need to take the other side of every bet — we match buyers with sellers and let the market set the price.

The result: customers get better odds, and we still run a profitable business. We've been profitable for multiple years — named "one of the most profitable per capita companies in Europe" by Corporate Rebels.

How we make money

Market making

We provide liquidity by quoting prices on both sides of a market. This earns us roughly 5% of customer trading volume on average.

Transaction fees

We charge approximately 25 basis points per trade on the exchange — among the lowest in the industry.

Where we're headed

As we expand into new asset classes, we'll add revenue from subscriptions, premium features, and interest on customer deposits.

How the flywheel works

Better prices attract more customers. More customers create more volume. More volume makes our market maker more efficient. And higher efficiency means we can reinvest in even better prices. This cycle has been compounding since 2008.
